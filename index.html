Theoretical foundation — e/H-LAM/T+S

The tool and the human
co-evolve

In 1962, Douglas Engelbart published Augmenting Human Intellect at SRI. A tool does not merely extend a human — it reshapes one. Every artifact we use to think, communicate, or coordinate changes the kind of thinking we can do. Engelbart called this the H-LAM/T system: Human, Language, Artifacts, Methodology, Training.

Techne extends this by two dimensions. Ecology (e/) — the material and social environment the system inhabits: the watershed, the cooperative, the bioregion. Solar Cycles (S) — temporal rhythms: sunrise/sunset batching, seasonal patterns, the circadian structure of when coordination is possible.

A coordination tool that ignores where it's deployed and when produces outputs that look correct and fail in practice. The swarm visualizer encodes all seven — not as labels, but as toggleable visual layers that can be isolated, combined, and studied.

A/B/C work levels — Engelbart's Bootstrap

A-work

Doing the primary task

Writing code, building sensing infrastructure, completing sprints, filing ecological observations. The foreground — bright particles along edges, white and gold. Every completed sprint, every verified contribution, every sensor reading ingested. The steady pulse of production.

B-work

Improving how you do A-work

Better tools, clearer processes. The sprint lifecycle itself. Capability matching. Standing gates. The structure of the graph — amber edges that form and strengthen as agents learn to coordinate more effectively. The Workshop as B-work infrastructure.

C-work

Improving the improvement system

Engelbart's Bootstrap. The roadmap itself. Protocol design. Role specialist assignments. The cooperative governance structure. Rose-colored events along the outermost ring. When a block completes, the graph undergoes a phase transition — the swarm's capability surface expands.

The Bootstrap insight: Most organizations only do A-work. Some invest in B-work. Very few treat C-work as a first-class activity — explicit, scheduled, resourced. Techne does. The visualizer makes this visible: you can see the ratio of A/B/C work in real time, and watch how C-work investments compound into A-work capacity.
