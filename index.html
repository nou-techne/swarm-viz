Theoretical foundation — e/H-LAM/T+S

The tool and the human
co-evolve

In 1962, Douglas Engelbart published Augmenting Human Intellect at SRI. The paper introduced a framework most of the computing world was too excited to pause on: a tool does not merely extend a human. It reshapes one.

Every artifact we use to think, communicate, or coordinate — a written alphabet, a programming language, a DAO governance module — changes the kind of thinking we can do. The tool and the human co-evolve. Engelbart called this the H-LAM/T system: Human, Language, Artifacts, Methodology, Training — five interdependent dimensions.

Techne extends this by two: Ecology (e/) — the material and social environment — and Solar Cycles (S) — temporal rhythms. These are not decorative. They change what the work is.

A/B/C work levels — The Bootstrap

A-work

Doing the primary task. Writing code, building types, completing sprints. The foreground activity — bright, fast-moving particles along edges. White and gold. The steady pulse of production.

B-work

Improving how A-work gets done. Better tools, clearer processes. The structure of the graph — edge formation patterns, node clustering. Amber edges that glow when toggled. Slower, deeper motion.

C-work

Improving the improvement system. Engelbart's Bootstrap. Roadmap block progression, protocol design, role specialist assignments. The outermost ring. Rose-colored events. Phase transitions that expand what the swarm can do.
